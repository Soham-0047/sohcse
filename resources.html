Eligibility & Timeline

Who can apply & when

GATE 2027 is expected to follow the same pattern as 2026. Dates will be officially announced by IIT around July 2026.

✅ Eligibility

  • Currently in 3rd+ year of BE/BTech/BSc (Research)
  • Completed Bachelor's in Engineering/Technology/Architecture (4+ years)
  • Master's degree holders in Science/Mathematics/Statistics/Computer Applications
  • International candidates from Bangladesh, Ethiopia, Nepal, Singapore, Sri Lanka, UAE
  • No age limit — can attempt any number of times

🎓 Qualifying Marks

  • General: 25 marks (or μ + σ, whichever higher)
  • OBC-NCL/EWS: 90% of General cutoff
  • SC/ST/PwD: 2/3 of General cutoff
  • Score valid for 3 years
  • Cutoff varies year-to-year based on difficulty

Two proven plans based on how much time you have. Adapt to your pace.

  • Aug–Sep 2026: Remaining — CO, CD, DL, Aptitude

Last 30 Days

🎯 Final Month Strategy

  • Take 8-10 full-length mock tests (timed)
  • Revise formula sheets and short notes daily
  • Solve last 5 years' PYQs chapter-wise
  • Don't start any new topic in last 15 days
  • Focus on weak areas identified in mocks
  • Sleep 7+ hrs, eat well — avoid burnout
  • Keep 1 day before exam fully light revision

💡 Pro Tips for GATE 2027

🎯

Aim for top 100, not just qualify

Aim high. If you target AIR 100, you'll likely land in top 1000. If you aim only to qualify, you might miss cutoff.

📝

PYQs > Everything

Solve last 15 years' PYQs at least 3 times. 70% of GATE questions are concept-repeats with new numbers.

📊

Mocks are non-negotiable

Take 30+ full mocks. Time management is what separates AIR 100 from AIR 1000.

🚫

Don't collect resources

Pick one textbook + one video lecture per subject. Finish them. Don't keep switching.

📓

Maintain short notes

Last 30 days, you'll only revise short notes. Start writing them from day 1.

😴

Sleep is study

7+ hours of sleep is non-negotiable. Burnout costs you months. Consistency > intensity.
